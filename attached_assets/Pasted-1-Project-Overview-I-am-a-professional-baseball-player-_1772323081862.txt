1. Project Overview

I am a professional baseball player building a web-based mentorship and player development platform for athletes ages 13–18.

The platform will focus primarily on:
	•	Athlete mentorship
	•	Mental development
	•	Recruiting education
	•	Leadership and discipline
	•	Structured player growth

It will also include limited video submission functionality for feedback and AI-assisted analysis.

The platform must be:
	•	Legally structured for minors (13–18 only)
	•	Parent-consent compliant
	•	Secure with video storage
	•	Scalable
	•	Built first as a web app (mobile responsive)

This is Phase 1 (MVP) with room for future expansion into a full mobile app.

⸻

2. Target Users

Primary Users:
	•	Baseball athletes ages 13–18

Secondary Users:
	•	Parents (required for account approval and payment)

⸻

3. Age Restrictions & Compliance Requirements

The platform must:
	•	Restrict access to users under 13 (hard block at signup)
	•	Require date of birth field at registration
	•	Require parent/guardian email for all users under 18
	•	Send automated parental consent email
	•	Require parent agreement to:
	•	Terms of Service
	•	Liability Waiver
	•	Privacy Policy

The system must store digital acceptance records.

⸻

4. Core Features (MVP Build)

A. User Registration & Authentication
	•	Email + password login
	•	DOB verification
	•	Parent email verification workflow
	•	User dashboard after approval
	•	Stripe subscription integration
	•	Account status (Active / Pending Parent Approval / Suspended)

⸻

B. Subscription & Payments
	•	Integrated with Stripe (my own Stripe account)
	•	Monthly subscription tiers:
	•	Tier 1 – Mentorship Access
	•	Tier 2 – Mentorship + Video Feedback Credits
	•	Tier 3 – Premium Access (Live sessions + priority review)
	•	Automatic recurring billing
	•	Subscription management panel
	•	Cancel / upgrade functionality

⸻

C. Athlete Dashboard

Upon login, athlete sees:
	1.	Progress Overview
	•	Modules completed
	•	Video submissions remaining (if applicable)
	•	Current development level
	2.	Weekly Mentorship Content
	•	Video lessons
	•	Downloadable resources
	•	Assignments/reflection prompts
	3.	Video Submission Portal (Tier-based access)
	•	Upload swing or training video
	•	Max file size control
	•	Upload limit based on subscription tier
	•	Status tracker (Submitted / Under Review / Feedback Posted)
	4.	Messaging / Announcements Section
	•	Platform updates
	•	Weekly insights from me

⸻

D. Content Delivery System

Structured development curriculum divided into:

Level 1 – Foundations
Level 2 – Competitive Mindset
Level 3 – Recruiting Blueprint

Each level contains:
	•	Video modules
	•	Progress tracking
	•	Locked progression until previous module complete

Admin must be able to:
	•	Upload new videos
	•	Add PDFs
	•	Schedule content release
	•	Edit curriculum

⸻

E. Video Feedback System

Athlete uploads video.

Admin (me) can:
	•	View video
	•	Leave written feedback
	•	Upload response video
	•	Mark as completed

Optional (Phase 2):
	•	AI-assisted mechanical tagging
	•	Auto-analysis summary
	•	Highlight key frames

For MVP:
Manual feedback system is acceptable.

⸻

F. Parent Portal

Parents should have:
	•	Access to athlete progress
	•	Payment management
	•	View completed modules
	•	View feedback history
	•	Download waiver/consent copies

Parents cannot edit athlete content but can manage account.

⸻

G. Admin Dashboard (Owner Panel)

Full admin backend must include:
	•	User management
	•	Subscription management
	•	Video review dashboard
	•	Content upload portal
	•	Metrics dashboard:
	•	Active users
	•	Churn rate
	•	Revenue
	•	Engagement data
	•	Ability to suspend accounts
	•	Ability to manually adjust subscription status

⸻

5. Legal Infrastructure Built Into System

The system must include:
	•	Required acceptance of:
	•	Terms of Service
	•	Privacy Policy
	•	Liability Waiver
	•	Parental Consent
	•	Timestamped digital signature storage
	•	Downloadable record for admin

⸻

6. Security Requirements
	•	Secure cloud video storage
	•	Role-based permissions (Athlete / Parent / Admin)
	•	SSL encryption
	•	Secure file uploads
	•	No public access to private videos

⸻

Reason:
Reduce moderation and liability risk.
